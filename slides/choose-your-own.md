### How does academia work?
<!-- .element: id="academic-ladder" -->
<!-- .slide: class="small-text"-->

The academic ladder in Denmark:

- <b>Postdoc</b>: Fixed-term (~6 months-3 years, typically). Common to take several temporary positions to build up your academic profile and experience before applying for a more senior role.
- <b>Assistant professor</b>: Fixed-term, but of higher seniority and salary than postdoc.
    - <b>Tenure-track</b>: After maximum six years, the employee is assessed and if they pass, they will be associate professor.
- <b>Associate</b>: Permanent position*
- <b>Professor</b>: Permanent position; a promotion for perticularly talented senior researchers.
- <b>Head of Department/Dean/Rector</b>: A posible adminstrative/political route for senior researchers.

Note:
Re-employment of an employee in several consecutive fixed-term positions is possible; however, such employment may not exceed eight years.

### What do I do day-to-day?
<!-- .element: id="academic-job" -->

The work of an academic is varied.

- <b>Research</b> (reading, coding, writing, reflecting etc.)
- <b>Collaborate</b> (email, lead, meet, plan)
- <b>Teach</b> (students courses)
- <b>Supervise</b> (Master/PhD students)
- <b>Admin</b> (Postdoc representative, committees, timesheets, safety courses etc)

This includes quite a bit of <b>travel</b> but mostly I work from my computer/desk at the office.

I am free to manage my own time<b>*</b>

### How to get a job in academia?
<!-- .element: id="job" -->

Well apply :)

Fresh out of a PhD you typically have two kinds of options for postdoctoral positions:

- Hired on a project lead by another PI: Someone has funding to hire you on a specific project with a goal in mind.
    - Most common
    - Conditions depend on your line manager, research location, research group, etc.
- Fellowship funding: You get your own funding and choose your host institution.
    - This is very competitive and requires a strong publication records and a clear research vision.
    - High prestige, very competitive.
    - Examples: Marie SkÅ‚odowska-Curie Postdoctoral Fellowships (EU), Harvard Society of Fellows (US), Carnegie Fellowships (US),  DFF International postdoctoral grants (DK), Carlsberg Internationaliserings stipends (DK), Carlsberg Reintegration stipends (DK), etc
- Industry/impact funding: Funding for research carried out in collaboration with a private company.
    - Examples: Innavation Fund Denmark (DK), some bigger private sector companies**.

Note:
- Your work situation depends a lot on your line manager, the research group/environment/institute, team members, etc

### Salary in Denmark

In Denmark, the salary in academia is set by the Collective Agreement for Academics in the State (Overenskomst negotiated between the State and your trade union).
This sets the basic salary, number of holidays, pension and much more is determined by this agreement.

### Work-life balance
<!-- .element: id="work-life" -->

<q>A healthy work-life balance allows everyone to excel at work and take care of their personal wellbeing outside of the office.</q>

- I have fun <b>now</b>

- I stopped taking every advice

- I try to be the best <b> whole </b> person I can

- I surround myself with real and honest friends

More great advice by Prof. Radhika Nagpal <a href="https://www.scientificamerican.com/blog/guest-blog/the-awesomest-7-year-postdoc-or-how-i-learned-to-stop-worrying-and-love-the-tenure-track-faculty-life/" target="_blank">in her blog post</a>

Note:
I remember to have fun now. I do fun science work stuff, I take time to learn new fun things even when I also have other priorities.

I stopped taking every advice. People, even with the best intentions, lie.
If you ask them what is important to succeed, people will tell you everything they did that they think helped them. Plus everything they wish they had done. Plus all the things their friend did. This list will be given without annotation and it is a list that no single person can ever accomlish. And you will receive that list, be in shock, then be depressed with a strong feeling of guilt and a desire to quit. 
And this person truely wanted you to success. So even with the best intentions, advice can be heartbreakingly dangerous.
So I have applied a heavy filter on how I take advice and I can only encourage you to give it some thought as well.

I try to be the best whole person I can. I am a scientist, but I am also a friend, a daughter, a partner, a nerd, a person with multitudes. So it is okay for me to not do work in the evening or weekends and to have holidays doing something else. When energy is replenished, it also makes me better at my work.

I build an environment of collegues and friends that value fun, whimsy, trust and honesty. People that I care about and that care about me and that makes every loss easier to handle and every win better.
